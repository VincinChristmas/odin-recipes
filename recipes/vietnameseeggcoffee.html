Vietnamese Egg Coffee

This is a cup of Vietnamese egg coffee

Though I have been to Vietnam many times, I have never had Vietnamese egg coffee there. I learned how to make this coffee when I was in China, actually, using a Chinese app that taught me how to cook food. It just so happens that they had many coffee recipes on the menu as well; Vietnamese egg coffee was one of them. I did research on the interenet and by asking around. I found out that this coffee is authentic. It is not popular all over Vietnam but is popular in one region in North Vietnam. Many people make this in a variety of ways. Here is mine recipe.

Ingredients

  • two egg yolks only(get rid of albumen--the egg white)
  • condensed milk
  • two espresso shots
  • Moka pot
  • Egg beater
  • a glass coffee cup

The Steps

  1. put two egg yolks in a bowl with 2 and a half tablespoons of condensed milk (can put a little less to make it less sweet)
  2. use an egg beater to mix the eggs and the condensed milk for about three minutes or until it is thick enough to float on water. Can test a drop of it on water.
  3. use the moka pot to make the two espresso shots. The amount that can be made in a moka pot is exactly two cups. If you don't know how to make a coffee in a moka pot, please read my moka pot instructions here
  4. pour the coffee into the cup
  5. pour the egg and condensed milk mixture on top of the coffee.
  6. sprinkle with cinnammon or a spice of your choice.
  7. can drink as is or enjoy the egg and condensed milk mixture with a spoon first.
